Ground Beef Spaghetti Sauce

A savory and affordable meal for the whole family. Feel free to customize to your liking and don't be afraid to add extra veggies to enhance the nutrition value. Goes well served with garlic bread and a side salad.

Description

There's nothing like classic spaghetti and meat sauce to bring people together around the dinner table. This homemade spaghetti sauce with ground beef recipe will satisfy all your comfort food cravings. Restaurant-worthy spaghetti sauce with ground beef is just a few steps away. You'll find the full recipe below, but here's a brief overview of what you can expect:

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, chopped
  • 1 small green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 28 oz can of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 16 oz can of tomato sauce
  • 1 6 oz can of tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Cook ground beef, onion, garlic, and green pepper in a large sauce pan over medium-high heat. Cook until beef is browned and veggies are tender. Drain grease from the pan.
  2. Stir in tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste into the pan. Season with basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add a teaspoon of sugar if you would like to counter some of the acidity of the tomatoes.
  3. Simmer for 1 hour.
  4. Add a few tablespoons of the water from the cooked pasta. This will help meld the flavors and help the sauce stick to the pasta.
